# Quality Evaluation Thoughts - Community AI Prompts

## Initial Misconceptions (To Review Later)
- Assumed paid = higher quality (incorrect assumption)
- Overvalued "expert validation" vs. community transparency  
- Underestimated Reddit and open community value
- Failed to prioritize transparency and verifiability

## Key Insights from User Feedback
- Transparency is more valuable than claimed expertise
- Community upvoting (like GitHub stars) provides verifiable quality metrics
- Open source communities often have better quality control than paid platforms
- Reddit's voting system is actually a robust quality indicator
- Verifiable metrics (downloads, stars, upvotes) > unverifiable claims

## Better Quality Indicators for Community Sources
- GitHub stars and fork counts
- Reddit upvotes and comment quality
- npm download statistics
- Transparent contribution history
- Open issue/discussion quality
- Community maintenance activity
- Version control and improvement tracking

## Questions to Explore Later

- What role does community discussion play in prompt refinement?
